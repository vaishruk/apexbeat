Editorial & academic policies

The standards that govern how content on this platform is written, referenced, and corrected. These policies exist so the education can be trusted and safely expanded.

Policies

Editorial Policy

This platform is an educational resource created by an Internal Medicine resident to help residents learn cardiology. Content is organized into three progressive levels and written to be visual, structured, and clinically useful. Every clinical statement is intended to reflect current professional guidelines and primary evidence.

Content is labeled as educational sample content. Sample material illustrates the format and reasoning of a topic and must be verified against current guidelines and local protocols before any clinical application.

Referencing Standards

References, citations, digital object identifiers (DOIs), and PubMed identifiers (PMIDs) are transcribed directly from source publications. They are never generated, inferred, or approximated.

  • Guideline recommendations cite the issuing organization, publication year, class of recommendation, and level of evidence.
  • Trial summaries report only results that appear in the verified primary publication.
  • Where verified sources have not yet been added, content is marked as sample or placeholder rather than presented as fact.

Corrections Policy

Errors are corrected promptly and transparently. When a substantive correction is made, the change and its date are noted. Readers who identify a possible error are encouraged to report it through the contact channel so it can be evaluated and, if warranted, corrected.

Contributors

The platform is created and maintained by Vaishnavi Sabesan, MD, Internal Medicine Resident.

Educational Disclaimer

This website is intended for medical education. It does not replace institutional protocols, specialist consultation, or individualized clinical judgment. Recommendations should be verified against current professional guidelines and local policies. Nothing on this platform establishes a clinician–patient relationship or constitutes medical advice for an individual patient.

Privacy

This initial version has no account system and no payment system. Progress indicators, bookmarks, and theme preference are stored locally in your own browser and are not transmitted to any server. No personal health information is collected. If analytics or contact features are added later, this policy will be updated to describe exactly what is collected and why.

Guideline sources

Clinical-society guideline recommendations are paraphrased and deep-linked to the official publication; the copyrighted figures themselves are not reproduced. Primary sources cited on the guideline cards include the 2025 ACC/AHA Acute Coronary Syndromes guideline, the 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure guideline, and the 2023 ACC/AHA/ACCP/HRS Atrial Fibrillation guideline (all published in Circulation).
